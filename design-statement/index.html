design statement

As an artist and graphic & motion designer,

I often find myself imagining how a static project I’m working on would look if it were part of a larger motion sequence, or how a frame I come across would look as an isolated still graphic.

How can I create action from a series of beautiful images flowing through time? Conversely, how can I convey the feeling of suspended time and action in a single frame? Many of the compositions and shapes I choose today are made as striking as possible while preserving the simplicity of the design. Dynamic angles, bold and erratic lines, and bright colors portray motion in a frozen medium.
